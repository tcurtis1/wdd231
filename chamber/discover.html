Discover Timbuktu

A Historic City of Culture and Commerce

Demographics of Timbuktu

Timbuktu, located in Mali, has a population of approximately 54,453 as of the last census in 2009. The city has experienced steady growth, with a diverse population including Tuareg, Songhai, and Arab communities. The majority of residents are engaged in trade, education, and tourism, reflecting Timbuktu’s historical role as a center of learning and commerce.

Cultural Highlights

Historical Sites

Visit the Sankore Mosque, a UNESCO World Heritage site, known for its role in Islamic scholarship during the 15th century.

Festivals

Experience the Festival au Désert, a celebration of Tuareg culture with music, dance, and camel races.

Markets

Explore the bustling markets of Timbuktu, offering traditional crafts, textiles, and local cuisine.

Explore Timbuktu

Economic Opportunities

Timbuktu’s economy thrives on trade, with its strategic location along historic trans-Saharan trade routes. Today, tourism plays a significant role, attracting visitors to its cultural landmarks. Local businesses, including artisans and merchants, contribute to a vibrant economy, supported by the Timbuktu Chamber of Commerce.

Visitor Information

  • Travel by air to Timbuktu Airport or by road via the Niger River.
  • Visit during the cooler months (November to February) to avoid extreme heat.
  • Explore attractions like the Djinguereber Mosque and the Ahmed Baba Institute of Higher Learning.
  • Respect local customs, including dressing modestly and asking permission before photographing people.
