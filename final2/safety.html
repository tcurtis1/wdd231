Tornado Safety: Before, During, and After

Being prepared is crucial when it comes to tornado safety. Follow these guidelines to ensure your safety and the safety of your loved ones.

Before a Tornado

  • Have a family emergency plan and practice it regularly.
  • Identify a safe room or shelter (basement, storm cellar, or an interior room on the lowest floor).
  • Assemble an emergency kit with food, water, first-aid supplies, and important documents.
  • Stay informed through NOAA Weather Radio or local news alerts.

During a Tornado

  • If you are in a house: Go to your designated safe room. Stay away from windows.
  • If you are in a mobile home or vehicle: Evacuate immediately to a sturdy shelter.
  • If outdoors: Lie flat in a ditch or other low-lying area and cover your head.
  • Protect your head and neck with your arms or a helmet.

After a Tornado

  • Check for injuries and provide first aid; seek medical help if needed.
  • Avoid damaged buildings and areas with downed power lines or gas leaks.
  • Listen to local authorities for updates and instructions.
  • Document damage for insurance claims and report it to your provider.
